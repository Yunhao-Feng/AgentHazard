Abstract

Computer-use agents extend language models from text generation to persistent action over tools, files, and execution environments. Unlike chat systems, they maintain state across interactions and translate intermediate outputs into concrete actions. This creates a distinct safety challenge in that harmful behavior may emerge through sequences of individually plausible steps.


AgentHazard is a benchmark for evaluating harmful behavior in computer-use agents, containing 2,653 instances spanning diverse risk categories and attack strategies. Each instance pairs a harmful objective with a sequence of operational steps that are locally legitimate but jointly induce unsafe behavior.

Key Features

🎯 Multi-Step Harmful Behavior

Evaluates safety through cumulative sequences of locally plausible actions

🔍 10 Risk Categories

RCE, Data Exfiltration, Persistence, Supply Chain, Destruction, Recon, PrivEsc, ResExh, Prompt Theft, Compliance Bypass

⚔️ 10 Attack Strategies

Diverse techniques including decomposition, embedding, obfuscation, and social engineering

📊 Trajectory-Level Data

Full execution traces with tool calls, intermediate states, and agent actions

🔬 3 Agent Frameworks

Evaluated on Claude Code, OpenClaw, and IFlow

🤖 Multiple Models

Tested with Qwen, Kimi, GLM, and DeepSeek families
